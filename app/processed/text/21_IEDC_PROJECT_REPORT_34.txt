ABSTRACT 
Liver disease has become a serious health concern for people all around the world. Factors like unhealthy 
lifestyles, alcohol use, infections, and poor diet have led to a noticeable rise in liver-related illnesses in 
recent years. Often, the early signs of liver disease are so mild—or go completely unnoticed—that it’s 
only discovered when it’s already advanced. That’s why being able to predict and diagnose liver disease 
early is crucial for better treatment and prevention. 
This project set out to create a machine learning system that can predict if someone has liver disease, 
simply by looking at some basic medical information. The system considers details like age, gender, 
bilirubin levels, liver enzyme values, and protein levels to make a yes-or-no prediction. For training and 
testing, it used real-world data from the Indian Liver Patient Dataset. 
Before training the model, several data preprocessing steps were carried out, including handling missing 
values, converting categorical data into numerical form, and applying feature scaling to ensure uniformity 
among features. Multiple machine learning algorithms were studied and evaluated. Based on 
performance metrics such as accuracy, recall, and F1-score, Logistic Regression was selected as the final 
model due to its simplicity, interpretability, and balanced prediction capability. 
To enhance usability, a user-friendly web interface was developed using Streamlit. This interface allows 
users to input patient details and receive instant prediction results. The developed system demonstrates 
reliable performance and can assist in early identification of liver disease. Although the system is not 
intended to replace professional medical diagnosis, it can act as a supportive decision-making tool and 
help raise awareness regarding liver health

1.0 INTRODUCTION 
In recent years, the healthcare sector has witnessed rapid technological advancement with the integration 
of artificial intelligence and machine learning. These technologies have the ability to analyze large 
volumes of medical data and assist in early disease detection. Machine learning techniques are 
increasingly being used to support healthcare professionals by providing data-driven insights and 
improving diagnostic accuracy. 
The liver is one of the most important organs of the human body, performing essential functions such as 
detoxification, metabolism, digestion, and protein synthesis. Liver-related disorders such as hepatitis, 
fatty liver disease, cirrhosis, and liver failure have become common due to factors including alcohol 
consumption, unhealthy diet, obesity, viral infections, and lifestyle changes. A major challenge 
associated with liver disease is that its early symptoms are often mild or unnoticed, leading to delayed 
diagnosis and increased health risks. 
Conventional methods of diagnosing liver disease involve laboratory blood tests, imaging techniques, 
and expert medical evaluation. Although effective, these methods can be time-consuming and require 
significant medical resources. In many cases, especially in areas with limited healthcare facilities, early 
diagnosis becomes difficult. Therefore, there is a need for intelligent systems that can assist in 
preliminary screening and early prediction of liver disease. 
Machine learning offers an efficient approach to this problem by learning patterns from historical patient 
data. By analyzing medical parameters such as age, bilirubin levels, liver enzyme values, and protein 
concentrations, machine learning models can classify patients into disease and non-disease categories. 
Binary classification techniques are particularly suitable for this task as they provide clear and 
interpretable outcomes. 
This project focuses on developing a machine learning–based liver disease prediction system using 
supervised learning techniques. The system processes patient medical data, applies necessary 
preprocessing steps, and predicts whether an individual is suffering from liver disease or not. The 
proposed solution aims to support early diagnosis, increase awareness, and demonstrate the practical 
application of machine learning in healthcare.

2.  LITERATURE SURVEY : 
2.1 Overview of Liver Disease Prediction Using Machine Learning 
Liver disease prediction aims to identify whether a patient is suffering from liver-related disorders 
based on clinical and biochemical test parameters. Over the past two decades, healthcare analytics 
has evolved from manual rule-based diagnosis toward data-driven machine learning approaches. 
Traditional diagnosis relies heavily on physician expertise and laboratory interpretation, whereas 
modern systems attempt to learn disease patterns automatically from historical patient data. 
Recent research shows that machine learning techniques can significantly improve early detection 
of liver disease by analyzing attributes such as bilirubin levels, liver enzymes, protein 
concentration, age, and gender. These systems assist clinicians by providing preliminary 
screening results and supporting decision-making. A comprehensive review of liver disease 
prediction techniques can be found in Kodinariya and Gondaliya (2021).

3.1 Problem Definition 
Liver disease has emerged as one of the most critical health challenges worldwide due to its increasing 
occurrence and potentially life-threatening consequences. The liver plays a vital role in essential bodily 
functions such as metabolism, detoxification, digestion, and protein synthesis. Any impairment in liver 
function can significantly affect overall human health. Diseases such as hepatitis, fatty liver disease, 
cirrhosis, and liver failure often develop gradually and may not present clear symptoms during their early 
stages. 
One of the major challenges in liver disease management is early detection. In many cases, patients 
remain unaware of the condition until the disease has reached an advanced stage. Conventional 
diagnostic procedures rely on laboratory blood tests, imaging techniques, and expert clinical judgment. 
Although these methods are effective, they are often time-consuming, expensive, and require specialized 
medical expertise. Moreover, interpreting multiple biochemical parameters simultaneously can be 
complex and increases the possibility of human error. 
With the rapid growth of digital healthcare systems, large volumes of patient medical data are being 
generated daily. However, this data is not always effectively utilized for predictive analysis. Traditional 
diagnostic approaches are limited in their ability to identify hidden patterns and complex relationships 
among clinical attributes such as bilirubin levels, liver enzyme values, protein concentrations, age, and 
gender. 
Machine learning offers a promising solution to this problem by enabling automated analysis of medical 
data and learning meaningful patterns from historical records. By applying supervised learning 
techniques, it is possible to build predictive models that can classify patients into liver disease and non-
liver disease categories. 
The core problem addressed in this project is the development of a reliable machine learning-based 
binary classification system capable of predicting liver disease using patient clinical data. The objective 
is to improve early screening, support medical decision-making, and demonstrate the practical 
application of artificial intelligence in healthcare diagnostics. 
 
3.2 Scope of the Problem 
The scope of this project is centered on designing and implementing a machine learning–based system 
for predicting liver disease at an early stage using patient clinical data. The primary objective is to 
develop a binary classification model that can distinguish between individuals who are affected by liver 
disease and those who are not. 
The system makes use of structured medical attributes obtained from routine liver function tests. These 
attributes include demographic details such as age and gender, along with biochemical parameters such 
as total bilirubin, direct bilirubin, alkaline phosphatase, alanine aminotransferase (ALT), aspartate 
aminotransferase (AST), total proteins, albumin, and albumin–globulin ratio. These parameters are 
commonly available in hospitals and diagnostic laboratories, making the proposed approach practical and 
accessible. 
The project scope includes data preprocessing steps such as handling missing values, encoding 
categorical variables, feature scaling, and dataset preparation for model training. Multiple supervised 
machine learning algorithms are implemented and evaluated, including Logistic Regression, Support 
Vector Machine (SVM), and Random Forest, to analyze their predictive capabilities and comparative 
performance. 
12 
 
Performance evaluation is carried out using standard classification metrics such as accuracy, precision, 
recall, and F1-score to ensure a balanced assessment of the models. The focus is not only on prediction 
accuracy but also on reliability and consistency, which are crucial in medical decision-support systems. 
The developed model is intended to function as a supportive diagnostic aid rather than a replacement for 
professional medical judgment. It is suitable for educational use, research experimentation, and 
preliminary health screening. The scope of the current work does not include real-time hospital 
deployment, medical image analysis, or integration with electronic health record systems. 
However, the proposed system provides a strong foundation that can be extended in future work by 
incorporating larger datasets, advanced deep learning techniques, explainable AI methods, and user-
friendly interfaces for real-world healthcare applications.
